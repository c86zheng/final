DESIGN WORK

Realm of the Gaze

This piece reflects my exploration of being observed and shaped by modern spaces. The lone figure, towering buildings, and watchful eyes reveal my search for meaning within a world both constructed and uncertain.

Shadow of street

In a street corner sliced by sunlight, human shadows fall and scatter across the scene.

Open Line, Open Mind

A minimalist icon set inspired by sex-positive principles, using one-stroke lines to express openness and the normalization of sexual desire. As a campus project, it was a bold exploration.

WRITING WORK

Hometown

“I drift between my hometown and distant lands, searching for myself amidst family constraints, the shadow of illness, and solitude. Escape brings freedom, yet it also makes me acutely aware of my powerlessness and the price of growing up.”

The Child Who Still Cries at the Gods’ Parade

This piece reflects on my childhood memories of deity parades during Lunar New Year and the quiet loneliness of studying abroad. Through distance, fear, and longing, I rediscover how deeply I still yearn for home and my mother’s warmth.
